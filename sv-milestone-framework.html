Canton Network Super Validator Accountability Framework

Rules and Guidelines for Weight Release and Maintenance

1. Weight Release Process

1.1 Milestone Delivery Review

Super Validators seeking weight release must schedule a presentation with the Accountability Committee following this process:

Pre-Submission Requirements:

  • Contact operations@sync.global to book a committee slot
  • Provide link to the original public CIP detailing the milestone and contact details of presenter / requested
  • Submit presentation materials 48 hours in advance

Presentation Framework:

  1. CIP Reference: Clear citation of the original CIP section detailing the specific milestone and deliverables
  2. Delivery date: A specific date as of which the Super Validator is claiming to have delivered the deliverables
  3. Milestone Demonstration: Live demonstration or comprehensive proof of milestone completion
  4. Weight Request: Explicit statement of the weight allocation associated with the milestone delivery
  5. Technical Review: For technical milestones, concurrent approval from the Technical Committee is required
  6. Precedent: If the applicant wishes, they may refer to our prior published decisions to ensure we remain consistent with them.  They should present clear citations to those decisions.

1.2 Committee Evaluation Criteria

The Accountability Committee will evaluate submissions based on:

  • Complete fulfillment of milestone acceptance criteria as defined in the original CIP
  • Quality and sustainability of the delivered solution
  • Documentation and operational readiness
  • Alignment with network objectives and institutional standards

1.2 3 Confidential Information and NDA Handling

For any milestone delivery that includes confidential information subject to a Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), the following process applies:

  • Confidential materials must be presented directly to the Accountability Committee.
  • The Foundation Executive Director or a designated Foundation representative must attend the review meeting to confirm handling expectations.
  • The SV and the Foundation must explicitly agree, during the meeting, on what information may be released publicly and what must remain confidential.
  • The Accountability Committee will complete its review of the milestone delivery based on the confidential materials presented.
  • The Tokenomics Committee will vote to accept or reject the milestone and associated weight release based on the Accountability Committee’s recommendation.
  • After public disclosure is permitted, the SV is responsible for releasing the approved materials, in accordance with agreed-upon disclosure terms.

2. Deadline Management and Penalty Framework

2.1 Deadline Evaluation

  • Effective Date: The critical date for performance of a deliverable is the date on which the work was finished, not the date on which it is presented to the Accountability Committee.  The Super Validator may present relevant evidence to establish the date upon which the work was finished.

2.2 Acceptable Delays

  • Events and Dependencies Outside the Super Validator’s Control: If delivery is delayed by a force majeure event or by a dependency fully outside the Super Validator’s control, the Super Validator must provide evidence supporting that claim, notify the Accountability Working Group at least 30 days before the original deadline or, if that is not possible, as soon as reasonably practicable after the event occurs, and present its case to the Accountability Working Group. The Tokenomics Committee will then consider the request to extend the deadline.
  • Linear Reduction Period: Following the original deadline, the weight associated with the milestone will reduce linearly from 100% to 0% over a period equal to the lesser of
  • 25% of the original delivery timeframe for that milestone, and
  • 90 days
  • Acceleration Bonuses: This linear reduction period does not apply to acceleration bonuses, which must still be earned strictly in accordance with their original timing requirements

2.3 Delay Handling and Weight Calculation

 On or Before Original Deadline:

  • Full weight release proceeds upon milestone completion
  • Acceleration bonuses, if any, are determined exclusively under their original terms and are not extended by the linear reduction period

 After Original Deadline but Within Linear Reduction Period:

  • Weight release may still proceed upon milestone completion, but the weight associated with the milestone is reduced on a straight-line basis according to the number of days elapsed after the original deadline
  • The linear reduction period is the lesser of 25% of the original delivery timeframe for that milestone and 90 days

Beyond Linear Reduction Period:

  • Weight associated with milestone is cancelled
  • If weight is reduced to 0, SV status is removed
  • SV can submit a new CIP with updated milestones and timelines
    Note: Worth thinking if in this case the SV is not allowed to be an SV or portion for a lengthy period of time. They already got a reward. Or they can reapply if they forfeit their reward so far?!
  • Previously completed milestones may be credited at committee discretion

Calculation

  • Calculation of Linear Reduction Period: For a milestone with original duration d days, the applicable Linear Reduction Period is
    l = min(ceil(0.25 * d), 90) 
    days, where     ceil means the value is rounded up to the next full day
  • Calculation of Releasable Weight: The releasable weight for a delayed milestone should be calculated as follows:

    Let:
    d = original milestone duration in days
    l = applicable linear reduction period in days = min(ceil(0.25 * d), 90)
    t = number of full days by which delivery is overdue
    w = original milestone weight

    Then:
    If t <= 0, releasable weight = w
    If 0 < t < l, releasable weight = w * (1 - t / l)
    If t >= l, releasable weight = 0

2.4 Missed / Failed Milestones

If a SV fails or partially meets the requirements of a milestone deliverable for their CIP, the unearned weight should be removed from the previous “ghost” SV beneficiary and reduce the total outstanding SV weight.  This should follow the following:

  • Unearned weight should only be removed after any applicable linear reduction periods
  • If a milestone is partially met and the SV cannot earn the remaining weight, the unearned weight should be removed upon granting the earned weight to the SV’s minting beneficiary regardless of any applicable linear reduction periods

3. Weight Maintenance and Renewal

3.1 Initial Vesting Period

  • Released weight automatically vests for 12 months from the date of committee approval
  • No additional review required during this period
  • SVs maintain full reward allocation based on their validated weight

3.2 Renewal Requirements

SVs will be invited to present to the Accountability committee continued operational and value creation commitment:

Renewal Process:

  • Documented Check‑in: Approximately 45–60 days before a Super Validator’s vesting period ends, the Accountability & Governance Committee will invite the SV to provide a brief update and confirm their desire to continue. The SV should summarize recent contributions and outline focus areas for the next period.
  • Automatic renewal: If the SV remains responsive, maintains operational reliability and continues to create value, the renewal will be granted automatically.
  • Structured escalation: If concerns arise (e.g., prolonged downtime or lack of engagement), the Accountability committee will issue a notification and offer assistance. A second notification will be issued if issues persist, followed by a final warning to then escalate to the Tokenomics committee for further action.

3.3 Renewal Outcomes

  • Auto Approvals: Full weight renewal for additional 12 months
  • Conditional during notification period: Partial weight renewal while waiting for responses to Accountability notifications
  • Escalate to the Tokenomics Committee for Review: If there is no response or concerns persist relating to the SV then the issue/s escalated to the Tokenomics committee to review.

3.4 Standard Reward Rate Guidance

  • For calculation and configuration purposes, reward rates will be expressed with precision (0.0001). For .00005 standard rounding is applied to become .0001. Any rates that require greater precision than must be explicitly approved through a formal governance decision.

  • Existing Super Validator commitments approved under prior terms will continue as approved unless modified through a formal CIP update.

  • This standard is intended to maintain fairness across participants and support long-term program sustainability.

4. Implementation and Communication

4.1 Immediate Actions

Existing SV Review:

  • Audit all current SVs against the above standards
  • Identify SVs with overdue milestones or expired vesting periods
  • Provide 30-day notice for required compliance actions

Support Framework:

  • Offer technical assistance to SVs at risk of slashing
  • Provide clear guidance on milestone completion requirements
  • Establish regular check-ins for SVs approaching deadlines

4.2 Legacy SV Management

  • Accountability committee will work towards bringing SVs without public CIPs into this Accountability Framework as soon as reasonably practicable
  • Accountability committee will need access to people and documents to understand the commitments made by and to legacy SVs

5. Committee Operations

5.1 Meeting Schedule

  • Regular weekly review sessions
  • This may be reduced if workload decreases
  • Ad-hoc sessions for urgent matters
  • Quarterly comprehensive SV portfolio review

5.2 Decision Making

  • Simple majority required for routine decisions
  • Two-thirds majority required for slashing actions
  • All decisions documented with clear rationale

6. Reporting and Transparency

6.1 Public Communication

  • Monthly summary of committee actions (anonymized where appropriate)
  • Annual report on SV performance and network contributions
  • Clear communication of framework changes and updates
  • All disclosures are subject to applicable NDAs and prior disclosure approval under Section 1.3.

6.2 SV Obligations

  • Quarterly progress reports during milestone development
  • Annual contribution summaries for renewal purposes
  • Prompt notification of material changes affecting milestone delivery

7. High Level process

  • Approved CIP with marked date on CIP in Github is marked active
  • Ghost SV created to hold unclaimed and unminted SV rewards
  • This is the start date for CC reward calculations
  • Work is done for those deliverables
  • Deliverables approved as complete by Accountability
  • Deliverables approved as complete by Tokenomics
  • Config changed to have SV rewards now go to owner SV for the appropriately approved weight
  • This date of config change used to calculate CC reward amount transfer from ghost to SV owner
  • This calculation must be done by 2 independent parties, the Foundation initiates IEU to run the python script and PixelPlex via their block explorer.
  • The partner should also calculate the rewards.
  • If the numbers match then they can go straight to Tokenomics for approval (via email)
  • If the numbers do NOT match then this should come to Accountability for review
  • Process repeats until all deliverables are completed and ghost validator no longer required
  • CIP moves to Final state
