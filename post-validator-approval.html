Post-Approval

Validator Onboarding Process after approval

This page covers what happens after validator approval, including DevNet practice, MainNet onboarding, whitelisting, onboarding secrets, and how to stay connected with the Global Synchronizer Foundation.

Note: If you are only interested in setting up a validator node, you can set up a node on DevNet for practice and then jump to MainNet. If you plan to build, test, or deploy an app, it is recommended that you run a node on DevNet, TestNet, and MainNet.
Note: DevNet is open to any whitelisted node. TestNet and MainNet require an invitation through a Super Validator sponsor.

Stay connected

Join the #validator-operations channel hosted by the Global Synchronizer Foundation using Slack Connect.

Your Slack workspace may allow you to browse to this channel, or you can ask your SV sponsor to send you an invitation.

To stay up to date with changes and announcements from the Global Synchronizer Foundation, consider joining these email lists:

  • main
  • cip-announce
  • tokenomics-announce
  • validator-announce

Create an account at groups.io, then go to lists.sync.global and sign up.

High-level steps

  1. Ask the Super Validators to whitelist a fixed IP on DevNet for your validator node.
  2. Spin up your node for DevNet.
  3. Request a DevNet onboarding secret via API call.
  4. Ask the Super Validators to whitelist your fixed MainNet IP for your validator node.
  5. Spin up your node on MainNet.
  6. Request a MainNet onboarding secret via Slack to your sponsoring SV.

Detailed onboarding steps

  1. Whitelist your IP for DevNet. Share your IP with your Super Validator sponsor. The sponsor will submit a request to the other Super Validators, and adoption typically takes 2 to 7 days.
  2. Spin up your DevNet node by following the setup documentation at docs.canton.network.
  3. Request a secret from your SV sponsor. Once 2/3 of the Super Validators have whitelisted your IP, you will be able to join DevNet and request an onboarding secret via API call.
  4. Request approval to join MainNet by asking a Super Validator to sponsor you and by completing the validator request form at canton.foundation/apply-to-set-up-a-validator-node.
  5. Your application will be reviewed by the Tokenomics Committee of the Global Synchronizer Foundation. Requests are accepted or rejected after one week, and accepted validators are announced on the tokenomics-announce mailing list.
  6. Whitelist your IP for MainNet. Share your MainNet IP with your Super Validator sponsor. As with DevNet, adoption by the other Super Validators typically takes 2 to 7 days.
  7. Spin up your MainNet node by following the MainNet setup documentation at docs.canton.network.
  8. Request a MainNet onboarding secret from your sponsoring SV through Slack. The same sponsor-based process applies for TestNet and MainNet.

Whitelisting verification

After you submit your IP for whitelisting, adoption can take up to 7 days because 2/3 of the Super Validators need to adopt the whitelist. The scripts below let you check how many Super Validators are already reachable from your IP.

DevNet verification script 
for url in \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.c7.digital \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.cumberland.io \
https://scan.sv-2.dev.global.canton.network.cumberland.io \
https://scan.sv.dev.global.canton.network.digitalasset.com \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.digitalasset.com \
https://scan.sv-2.dev.global.canton.network.digitalasset.com \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.fivenorth.io \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.sync.global \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.lcv.mpch.io \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.mpch.io \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.orb1lp.mpch.io \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.proofgroup.xyz \
https://scan.sv.dev.global.canton.network.sv-nodeops.com \
https://scan.sv-1.dev.global.canton.network.tradeweb.com
do
  printf "%s: " "$url"
  resp=$(curl -fsS --ipv4 --connect-timeout 10 --max-time 20 "$url/api/scan/version" 2>/dev/null) \
    || { echo "unreachable"; continue; }
  echo "$resp" | jq -r '.version // "no version"'
done
