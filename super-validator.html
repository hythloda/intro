Super Validators

Super Validators help govern and coordinate the Global Synchronizer.

Super Validators play a central role in how changes are proposed, reviewed, approved, and adopted across the Canton Network. Participation requires a CIP, and CIP-0000 is the core process document that defines how those proposals move from idea to adoption.

Important: Super Validator participation requires working through the Canton Improvement Proposal process defined in CIP-0000.

Why Super Validators are useful

  • They provide a structured governance path for changes that affect the Global Synchronizer, both onchain and offchain.
  • They help create trust through open discussion, shared review, and transparent decision-making rather than ad hoc coordination.
  • They vote on proposed CIPs, and approvals require a 2/3 majority in favor.
  • They help ratify changes onchain when adoption steps are required, which turns Approved proposals into Final ones.
  • They give the network a consistent operating model for protocol evolution, governance updates, tokenomics decisions, and process changes.

How to think about CIP-0000

CIP-0000 is the starting point for understanding how proposals move through the Canton governance process. It explains how ideas are discussed, refined, sponsored, voted on, and adopted.

  • Read CIP-0000 if you want to understand the official proposal path.
  • Use it when preparing a governance, standards, tokenomics, process, or informational proposal.
  • Work with the community and CIP editors rather than trying to navigate the process alone.

What to do next

If you are serious about the Super Validator path, the best next step is to read the full CIP-0000 document and use it as the source of truth for process details.

For the milestone-based operating framework, review the dedicated SV milestone framework page, which syncs daily from the source document.

Quick start

  1. Read CIP-0000.
  2. Discuss your idea with the community on cip-discuss.
  3. Prepare your proposal in the cips repository.
  4. Work toward sponsor support and the formal process described in CIP-0000.
